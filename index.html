March 18, 2026 • Pre-Tournament Edition • 0 of 63 Games Played
The madness begins tomorrow. Brackets are locked. Rosters are set. And somewhere, right now, a guy who drafted two 6-seeds is Googling "can you trade in a fantasy bracket pool." Here's where everyone stands before the first ball is tipped.
1
The Green Machine
Lia
Michigan (1) Florida (1) Texas (11) Northern Iowa (12) Santa Clara (10) High Point (12) Queens (15) Wright St. (14)
Lia walked out of draft night with both Michigan and Florida — the betting favorite and the defending national champion — and nobody seems to be talking about it. Michigan (+325 to win it all) is the most complete team in the field, and Florida is a 1-seed with the institutional memory of cutting down nets last April. Those two alone could generate points deep into the second weekend, and Texas as an 11-seed adds first-round upset juice on top. Northern Iowa, a popular Cinderella pick with the 24th-ranked KenPom defense, is a sneaky bonus. This roster has the highest floor in the league and one of the highest ceilings. The Green Machine isn't flashy. It's just terrifyingly well-constructed.

Purple Reign
Bob1
Purdue (2) Illinois (3) St. John's (5) SMU/Miami OH (11) Hofstra (13) Kennesaw St. (14) North Dakota St. (14) Saint Louis (9)
The sleeper pick to win the whole thing, and it all hinges on one beautiful heist: St. John's is seeded as a 5 but plays like a 2. Nate Silver's COOPER model thinks the Johnnies are a legitimate top-8 team that the committee treated like a mid-major. Rick Pitino just won the Big East tournament and his team is going to score FDM points like a 5-seed while playing like a title contender. Pair that with Purdue (most efficient offense in America, Braden Smith about to break Bobby Hurley's all-time assists record) and Illinois (22-1 title odds before the tourney), and Purple Reign has three teams that could realistically reach the Elite Eight. SMU as an 11-seed adds a cherry on top. This is the roster that could look genius by Sunday night.

3
Red Rocket
Luke
Duke (1) VCU (11) Michigan St. (3) South Florida (11) UCLA (7) Villanova (8) Furman (15) Siena (16)
The Red Rocket is built the way FDM should be built: an elite anchor in Duke (the #1 overall seed, Cameron Boozer is a walking bucket), two 11-seeds in prime upset spots, and Tom Izzo doing Tom Izzo things in March. VCU might be the single best value pick in the entire draft — they face a UNC team that lost Caleb Wilson to a broken thumb, went 5-3 without him, and got bounced from the ACC Tournament by Clemson. The Rams have won 16 of their last 17 and VCU's Terrence Hill Jr. is about to light up a UNC defense ranked 242nd in opponent 3-point percentage. South Florida vs. Louisville is another coin flip. The concern? Duke is running a seven-man rotation with Caleb Foster out for the tournament and Ngongba questionable. That's a fatigue timebomb. If Ngongba plays, this is a top-2 roster. If Duke flames out early, the Rocket stalls on the launchpad.

Boom or Bust
4
Agent Orange
Joe
UConn (2) Arkansas (4) Virginia (3) Saint Mary's (7) Texas Tech (5) Hawaii (13) Miami FL (7) Tennessee St. (15)
Agent Orange is the steady, respectable roster that won't blow up the group chat on Thursday but could quietly be in first place by Monday. UConn at 25-1 is attracting sharp money at every sportsbook in America, because when you've got Dan Hurley and two national titles in the last three years, you don't fade that in March. Arkansas has won 13 of its last 15 behind Darius Acuff Jr., who might be the best player in the West region. Virginia is a 3-seed. Texas Tech is solid. And Hawaii — yes, Hawaii — runs a bizarre no-help, one-on-one defense that ranks 43rd nationally in KenPom efficiency. If the Rainbow Warriors pull the 13-over-4 upset, that's 25 FDM points in one game. Joe's roster doesn't have the upside fireworks of some others, but it's deep, well-rounded, and has no obvious disaster matchups. The Toyota Camry of FDM rosters: it just works.

Quietly Dangerous
5
Sa(L)mon Says
Chad
Arizona (1) Alabama (4) Kansas (4) Nebraska (4) Louisville (6) North Carolina (6) Texas A&M (10) Lehigh/PVAMU (16)
On paper, Arizona (+400, healthiest 1-seed, CBS experts' most popular title pick) should carry this roster to the promised land. And Alabama's offense averages 91.7 points per game, which is just obscene. Kansas has the potential #1 NBA Draft pick in Darryn Peterson. There's real talent here. But then you look at the 6-seeds and wince. Louisville is staring down South Florida in a game Vegas basically calls a toss-up, and North Carolina without Caleb Wilson just lost to Clemson in the ACC Tournament and looked like a team ready to be put out of its misery. Oh, and Alabama guard Aden Holloway (16.8 PPG) was just arrested this week after police allegedly found over two pounds of marijuana at his residence. Is that a lot? Honestly, we have no idea. We're pretty boring when it comes to pot. Team Orange will have to opine on that topic. What we do know is that it's not great for Chad's second-best team heading into Thursday. In FDM, 6-seeds are the biggest trap in the draft — they're as likely to make the Final Four as 7-seeds and half as likely as 8-seeds, which is just disrespectful to the concept of seeding. Chad is basically betting that his 1-seed and three 4-seeds generate enough deep-run points to overcome the dead weight. It's possible. But those 6-seeds put the hard "L" in SaLmon.

6-Seed Anxiety
6
Tickled Pink
Lucas
Houston (2) Gonzaga (3) Wisconsin (5) McNeese (12) Missouri (10) Cal Baptist (13) Akron (12) LIU (16)
Tickled Pink? Not yet. But Lucas might be by the end of the first weekend, and it all hinges on one beautiful setup: Houston (12-1 title odds) is a legitimate monster — last year's championship game runners-up brought back their core and added Kingston Flemings, a lock top-10 NBA pick. And here's the kicker: the South regional rounds are being played in Houston, giving the Cougars a borderline home-court advantage through the Elite Eight. Gonzaga is a 3-seed with that classic Gonzaga "we'll be fine in March" energy. But after those two, it gets quiet. Wisconsin is interesting, Missouri gets a pseudo-home game in St. Louis, and McNeese has 12-over-5 upset potential. Cal Baptist has a top-50 defense but an offense ranked 191st. This roster needs Houston to go on a war path and Gonzaga to back it up, because the bottom half isn't producing fireworks.

Houston or Bust
7
Blue Flu
Box
Iowa St. (2) Vanderbilt (5) Iowa (9) Utah St. (9) Ohio St. (8) Troy (13) Georgia (8) Idaho (15)
Iowa State is a real-deal title contender at 18-1, and TJ Otzelberger's crew is the best defensive team in the Midwest. Vanderbilt is a good 5-seed. After that? Box has assembled the most aggressively mid roster in FDM history. Two 8-seeds. Two 9-seeds. A 13 and a 15. In a normal bracket pool that's fine. In FDM, where 8-seeds and 9-seeds are historically mediocre point producers, you're looking at a roster where six of your eight teams are fighting for table scraps. Iowa State needs to go to the Final Four — at minimum — for this roster to have any chance. It's the FDM equivalent of showing up to a potluck with one really nice bottle of wine and six bags of store-brand chips.

Mid-Major Energy
8
Tarnished Silver
Bob2
Kentucky (7) Tennessee (6) BYU (6) UCF (10) Clemson (8) Penn (14) TCU (9) Howard/UMBC (16)
We don't want to be mean. We really don't. But somebody has to be last, and Bob2's roster reads like a list of teams you pick to lose in the second round of every bracket you've ever filled out. Two 6-seeds (Tennessee and BYU) — the biggest draft trap in FDM. No team in the top-20 of title odds. Kentucky is the headliner at 7-seed, which tells you everything. The lone upside play is BYU itself, where AJ Dybantsa (the nation's leading scorer at 25.3 PPG, possible #1 NBA pick, First Team All-American) has been dropping 40-pieces in the Big 12 Tournament — but the Cougars went 9-9 in league play, lost co-star Richie Saunders to a torn ACL, and are wildly inconsistent. If Dybantsa goes nuclear and BYU makes a Sweet 16 run as a 6-seed, that's enormous FDM value from a spot nobody expects it. Penn has former Duke forward TJ Power, which is a nice story, but it's not a path to points. This is the Silverback Gorilla without the gorilla. Just the silver, slowly tarnishing. Bob2 needs Dybantsa to channel his inner Jimmer Fredette, and even then, it might not be enough.
